Why the pen matters

A photo shows the result.
The pen feels the process.

Four of the twenty factors describe *how* the hand moves, speed, pressure, flow and pen-lifts. These live in the motion of writing, not the marks left behind. The Battu measures them directly with a 16-axis sensor array at 208 Hz.

16 axes · 208 Hz · Kalman-filtered

Why a photo isn't enough

A scan is a perfect record of the finished page. But two writers can produce an identical-looking line in completely different ways, one relaxed and fluent, one slow and pressing hard. That difference is invisible in a photo, and it is exactly what determines whether writing is comfortable and sustainable.

📷 A photo can measure

From the marks on the page
  • Letter size, spacing & margins
  • Baseline, slant & line straightness
  • Loop closure & letter shape

✍️ Only the pen can measure

From the motion of the hand
  • How fast and how evenly you write
  • How hard you press, moment to moment
  • Whether strokes flow or stop-start

The four factors the pen measures

These make up the Dynamics section (20% of the overall score). In photo mode they are honest estimates; with the pen they become measured, and far more accurate.

Factor 13 · Dynamics

Speed Consistency

How steady your writing pace is, stroke to stroke, measured from the pen's velocity, not guessed from ink thickness.

Why it matters to youEven speed is the heart of fluent, effortless writing. Writers who speed up and slow down erratically tire faster and make more slips. Smoothing your pace makes writing feel easier and look neater, at the same time.
Measured: per-stroke peak velocity → consistency(velocity) over the whole sample.
Factor 14 · Dynamics

Pressure Consistency

How evenly you press the pen, captured live by the tip force sensor in the nib.

Why it matters to youPressing too hard or unevenly causes hand fatigue and pain, the number-one reason writing becomes uncomfortable. A steady, relaxed force lets you write longer without aching, and keeps line weight clean and consistent.
Measured: per-stroke mean force → consistency(force); a photo can only see ink darkness.
Factor 15 · Dynamics

Stroke Continuity

Whether your strokes connect and flow, or break up into many small pieces, read from the pen-contact signal.

Why it matters to youFlowing strokes are faster and more automatic. When writing connects smoothly, the hand thinks less about forming each letter and more about ideas. Choppy, broken strokes slow you down and make notes harder to keep up with.
Measured: pen-contact segments per word → connectivity vs target.
Factor 16 · Dynamics

Pen-Lift Frequency

How often the pen leaves the page, an event that exists only in time, and so is impossible to see in a still image.

Why it matters to youFrequent, unnecessary lifts are a hidden brake on writing speed. Each lift is a tiny pause that adds up. Reducing them is one of the fastest ways to write more fluently and finish faster, something only the pen can show you.
Measured: pen-up events ÷ characters → lifts_per_char.
The big idea: the pen turns handwriting from a picture into a recording. That's why the same four factors jump from "estimated" to "measured" the moment you use the Battu, and why they're the truest guide to whether your writing is becoming fast and efficient.

Clean signals you can trust

Raw sensors are noisy. A Kalman filter fuses the readings and removes jitter in real time, so the numbers behind your score reflect your hand, not the electronics.

Raw → Kalman-filtered

The filter predicts where each signal should be, then gently corrects toward the new reading, smoothing spikes while staying responsive. You see the smooth line live on the capture screen.

The faint line is the raw sensor; the bold line is what the analysis uses.
