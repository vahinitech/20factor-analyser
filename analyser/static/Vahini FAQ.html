About handwriting itself

Does handwriting still matter when everything is typed?
Yes, because typing and handwriting do different jobs. Forming letters by hand forces the brain to encode each shape, which research repeatedly links to stronger letter recognition, memory and comprehension than typing. And practically: in India, classwork and exams from KG to PG are handwritten, board papers, entrance descriptives, university booklets. Legible writing remains a scoring skill. See the companion page Why Handwriting Matters.
At what age should handwriting be "fixed"?
There is no deadline. The foundation years (4–8) are the easiest time to build habits, but spacing, sizing and slant respond to focused practice at any age, adults included. What changes with age is the drill mix, not the possibility.
Is bad handwriting just laziness?
Usually not. Writing is a brain–hand coordination loop: recall the letter, plan the strokes, drive the muscles, correct from feedback. Messy output normally means one part of that loop needs training, grip, pressure, rhythm or letter memory. Occasionally persistent struggle signals a learning difference (dysgraphia, dyspraxia, dyslexia) that deserves professional attention, not blame.
Cursive or print, which should a child use?
Either, consistently. Coaches care less about which style and more about not mixing the two mid-page, because switching breaks rhythm and looks uneven. The Vahini report checks exactly this on its Letter-Level Findings page.
Can Vahini detect dyslexia or dysgraphia?
No, and it never claims to. The report measures handwriting quality and shows where the writing struggles. Diagnosis of any condition needs a qualified professional. What the report provides is an objective, dated record that a therapist or psychologist can use as supporting evidence.

Algorithms & AI

How does the analysis actually work?
A computer-vision pipeline runs on your photo, in your browser: it converts the image to ink-vs-paper, finds every letter, groups letters into words and lines, fits the baseline each line sits on, then measures size, spacing, slant, margins and more, 20 factors in all. Each measurement is compared to a published target band to make a 0–10 score. Full plain-language detail: computer-vision-algorithms.md.
Is it AI guessing my score?
No. Most factors are deterministic measurements, the same photo always produces the same numbers, like a ruler. AI (machine learning) is reserved for the few jobs it's genuinely better at: reading the words (text recognition), judging letter-shape similarity, and, in future, calibrating overall legibility to coach ratings. The report labels every factor's confidence honestly: Measured, Estimated, or awaiting the Battu.
How does AI help at all, then?
Three ways:
  • Recognition, reading what was written so the report can check words, spelling and punctuation against the passage;
  • Shape judgement, comparing your letterforms to ideals and telling look-alikes apart (a/o, n/h);
  • Personalisation, choosing which drills fit your weakest measured factors and forecasting your improvement curve.
The scores themselves stay measurement-first, so they're explainable and repeatable.
What is the Battu and why does it matter?
A sensor pen (Patent 584433) with two motion sensors (one with a built-in magnetometer) and a tip force sensor, 16 signals at 208 readings per second. It measures what no photo can: pressure, speed, stroke flow and pen lifts, the "hidden half" of handwriting. In photo mode those four factors are honestly left unscored; with the pen they become true measurements.
Why are some factors marked "Not scored" on my report?
Because they live in the motion of the hand (pressure, speed, pen-lifts, stroke order), and a still photo cannot see motion. Rather than invent a number, the report leaves them blank and tells you the typical target, capture a session with the Battu to measure them precisely.
How accurate is the analysis on equations, diagrams or mixed pages?
The engine is calibrated for handwritten prose. It detects the document type (prose, short answers, equations, diagrams, mixed printed+handwritten), excludes printed text and figures, and prints an honest accuracy note on the report. For assessment, a few lines of normal sentences works best.

Drills & improvement

How are my drills chosen?
From your own measurements, never a generic list. The engine takes your weakest reliably-measured factors (estimates and pen-pending factors are excluded), maps each to the drill that trains it (slant rails, oval rolls, spacing rhythm, page framing, pressure waves), and prescribes at most three, because focused practice beats a long list. Each drill carries a "because" sentence tracing back to your measured value.
How long until handwriting improves?
With ~10 minutes, 3× a week of the prescribed drills, most writers see measurable movement in 2–4 weeks, improvement is fastest early (a normal learning curve), which is exactly what the report's forecast page models. Re-scan to watch the real curve against the projection.
Why only three drills? My child has many weak areas.
Adherence. Coaching practice and basic behaviour research agree: a few focused interventions get done; a long list gets abandoned. Weak factors also travel together, fixing size evenness usually lifts legibility and neatness for free. After a re-scan, the prescription rotates to the next priorities.
Does the score guarantee improvement?
No score does. The loop does: measure → practise the matched drills → re-measure. The forecast assumes consistent practice and says so plainly. What the report guarantees is that you're practising the right things, with evidence.

For coaches, institutes, teachers & parents

How does Vahini help a handwriting coach?
It does the measuring so you can do the coaching:
  • An objective, repeatable baseline per student, with evidence cropped from their own page;
  • A Letter-Level Findings page that checks what you check: style mixing, stray capitals, letter-shape consistency, punctuation;
  • A ready prescription you can endorse, an instructor notes & sign-off block, and your logo on the cover;
  • Re-scan deltas that prove your course works to parents, your best marketing.
How does an institute or school use it at scale?
Standardise one reference passage per intake, scan each student (a minute per child at a stall or in class), and you get a comparable, dated record across the cohort. Re-test each term: the section-level deltas show programme impact, and the one-page Scorecard is the parent-communication unit. Co-branding and bulk practice sheets are supported.
What does a teacher get that marking can't give?
Specificity and time. Instead of "improve your handwriting" in the margin, the report says which two habits to fix and shows the child's own letters as proof. Ten seconds of reading replaces guesswork, and the certificate page gives younger children a reason to care.
I'm a parent with no handwriting knowledge. Can I use this?
That's exactly who the report is written for. Plain-language verdicts ("your letters range from very small to quite tall in the same word"), a maximum of three drills with pictures and arrows, and a star-certificate for the child. Ten minutes, three times a week, and re-scan in a few weeks. For tailored help: go@vahinitech.com.
Can adults use Vahini for themselves?
Yes. The Individual mode adapts the tone, no mascots, same measurements. Adults typically chase comfort and speed (pressure, rhythm, spacing) more than school-neatness, and those are exactly the factors the Battu measures best.

Privacy & trust

Where does my child's handwriting photo go?
In the demo, nowhere, the analysis runs entirely in your browser, on your device. Nothing is uploaded unless you explicitly connect a recognition server or share the PDF yourself.
Is this a medical or psychological assessment?
No. Every report carries the same disclaimer: it is for handwriting improvement, education and skill-building only, not a diagnosis of health, intelligence, personality or any condition. Vahini also deliberately rejects graphology-style personality reading; it is not scientifically supported.
Why should I trust the scores?
Because you can check them. Every factor shows the measured value, the target band, the sample size it was based on, and, for most factors, a crop of your own writing showing exactly what was measured. The methods are published in plain language (ARCHITECTURE.md, computer-vision-algorithms.md), built on peer-reviewed foundations (Otsu 1979; Bradley & Roth 2007; Schomaker 1990, 1993).
