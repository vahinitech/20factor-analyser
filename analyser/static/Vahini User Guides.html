Every report, at a glance

The same building blocks appear in every report. Learn these six and you can read any Vahini report.

Overall /100

The headline score

A weighted blend of all four sections. It is a starting line to improve from, not a grade or a verdict.

Section bars

Four families

Structure, Spatial, Dynamics and Style. The shortest bar is simply where the most room to grow is.

Measured

Computed from the sample

Geometry the engine measured directly, spacing, baseline, slant, size, margins, loops.

Needs the pen

Best with the IMU pen

Speed, pressure and pen-lifts. From a photo these are estimates; the Battu measures them.

Estimated

A transparent blend

Legibility & character distinction, built from the basics, with the letters to target named for you.

Forecast

The road ahead

A projection of your score, fluency and writing speed after a few weeks of the prescribed drills.

For students

Your handwriting, leveling up

Think of your report like a game character sheet. You have strengths already, and a few skills to train. Here's how to play.

you don't need perfect, you need a little better each week

Find your score and your superpowers

Look at the big number, then the green "Real strengths" box. Those are things you already do well, be proud of them!

Spot your one focus area

The "Where to focus" box lists what to practise. Don't try to fix everything, pick the very first one.

Do your drill prescription

Turn to "Your drill prescription." Each drill is drawn out with arrows. Trace it slowly, just 5–10 minutes, three times a week.

Check the forecast, then re-test

The forecast shows how much you can grow. After a week or two, get scanned again and watch your number climb!

Do this

  • Practise one drill at a time, slowly
  • Keep your sheets to see your progress
  • Celebrate every point you gain

Don't worry about

  • Comparing to friends, this is your race
  • Being perfect, neat-enough is the goal
  • The science words, just follow the arrows
Remember: this report is about helping your handwriting flow better. It does not say anything about how smart you are or anything about your health, it's just about the writing on the page.
For parents & guardians

Supporting the journey

The report gives you an objective, encouraging picture of your child's handwriting and a small, doable practice plan. Here's how to use it without pressure.

Start with strengths, out loud

Read the green strengths box together first. Naming what they do well builds the confidence that makes practice stick.

Understand the one or two focus areas

The focus box and section bars show where to grow. These are skills to build, not problems, most are about evenness and rhythm.

Make the drills a tiny routine

Ten minutes, three times a week, beats an hour once. The prescription page tells you exactly which drills and why.

Re-test to see real progress

The forecast is an estimate; a re-scan in a few weeks turns it into a real, motivating before-and-after.

Helpful habits

  • Keep it light and positive, praise effort
  • Good light, comfy grip, relaxed hand
  • Use the printable practice sheets

Please keep in mind

  • This is not a diagnosis of any kind
  • It does not assess intelligence or personality
  • For learning concerns, see a qualified professional
If something worries you, for example you suspect a motor or learning difficulty, this report is not the right tool to answer that. Treat it purely as practice guidance and consult a teacher or specialist. For tailored support, write to go@vahinitech.com.
My child scored lower than I expected. Is that bad?

No. A lower number just means more of the easy wins are still ahead, the forecast usually shows the biggest gains for exactly these writers.

How often should we re-test?

Every 2–4 weeks is plenty. Handwriting changes gradually; frequent re-tests can feel like pressure.

For individual writers

Self-paced, self-measured

You don't need perfect handwriting, you need handwriting that feels easy and looks like you. The report tells you precisely which few things move the needle.

Read the summary and section bars

Two minutes tells you your overall level and which of the four families is holding the score back.

Trust the prescription's logic

The drill page explains *why* each exercise was chosen from your own measurements, work only those; skip what's already strong.

Use the forecast to set a target

It estimates your score, fluency band and words-per-minute after eight weeks. Pick a realistic goal and a re-test date.

Re-scan and compare

Keep each report. The trend across re-tests is the most motivating part of the whole system.

Where to start

  • Fix evenness and rhythm first, the rest follows
  • Short, frequent practice beats marathons
  • For dynamics, the IMU pen gives the truest read

Make it stick

  • Same time, same place, same 10 minutes
  • Warm up the hand; keep a relaxed grip
  • Re-test monthly and watch the curve

For institutes & coaches

Endorse the prescription & sign off
